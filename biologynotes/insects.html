Insects of Economic Importance

1. Cockroach (Periplaneta americana)

The common species of cockroach in the tropics is Periplaneta americana. This insect is most active at night and hides in dark places during the day.

Classification

KingdomAnimalia
PhylumArthropoda
ClassInsecta
OrderDictyoptera
FamilyBlattidae
GenusPeriplaneta
Speciesamericana

Characteristics of Order: Dictyoptera

  • Mouthparts of the biting type
  • Presence of broad membranous outer wings
  • Long and thread-like antennae
  • Presence of anal cerci
  • Long and tough tegmina
  • Metamorphosis is incomplete

Habitat

Cockroaches live in dark, dirty, and damp places, such as pipes that carry sewage, crevices in walls, and cupboards. They are nocturnal.

Adaptations to the Environment

  • Dorsally flattened bodies for narrow spaces
  • Dark brown color for camouflage
  • Smooth and greasy body to escape predators
  • Long antennae for sensing the environment
  • Swift running ability
  • Omnivorous diet for survival
  • Nocturnal behavior for safety

External Features

Identification of Sex

MaleFemale
Narrow abdomenBroader abdomen

Has styles on the 9th segmentLacks styles on the 9th segment

Note: Other insects in the order Dictyoptera include locusts, crickets, praying mantis, and grasshoppers.

2. The House Fly (Musca domestica)

The house fly, Musca domestica, is commonly found in dirty places such as toilets, dustbins, and manure heaps.

Classification

KingdomAnimalia
PhylumArthropoda
ClassInsecta
OrderDiptera
FamilyMuscidae
GenusMusca
Speciesdomestica

Characteristics of Order: Diptera

  • Single pair of wings
  • Second pair of wings forms halteres or balancers
  • Short antennae
  • Suctorial and piercing mouthparts
  • Important vectors for diseases

Habitat

House flies are commonly found in filthy places such as toilets, dustbins, and manure heaps.

3. The Mosquito

Mosquitoes are major vectors for several diseases, such as malaria and dengue fever. The main genera include Anopheles, Culex, and Aedes.

Classification

KingdomAnimalia
PhylumArthropoda
ClassInsecta
OrderDiptera
FamilyCulicidae
GenusAnopheles, Aedes, Culex

External Features

  • Long slender legs
  • Proboscis for piercing and sucking
  • Membranous wings
  • Halteres for balance

Habitat

Mosquitoes thrive in tropical areas and are primarily found near stagnant water sources.

Diseases Spread by Mosquitoes

  • Malaria
  • Dengue fever
  • Yellow fever
  • Elephantiasis

4. The Honey Bee (Apis mellifera)

The honey bee, Apis mellifera, is a social insect and plays a vital role in pollination.

Classification

KingdomAnimalia
PhylumArthropoda
ClassInsecta
FamilyHymenoptera
GenusApis
Speciesmellifera

External Features

  • Prominent compound eyes
  • Short segmented antennae
  • Proboscis for feeding and construction
  • Pollen baskets on the hind legs

Roles in the Hive

  • Queen: Lays eggs
  • Drone: Fertilizes the queen
  • Worker: Collects food and protects the hive
