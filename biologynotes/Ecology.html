Ecology

Introduction

Ecology is the study of living organisms in relation to their environment; i.e., the study of interactions between living things and their environment. The organisms and the environment depend on each other for resources. For example, plants need carbon dioxide and water to manufacture food for animals to feed on.

Branches of Ecology

  • Autecology: Study of individual species of plants and animals.
  • Synecology: Study of communities (plants and animals living under similar conditions).

Key Terms in Ecology

  • Environment: Immediate surroundings of an organism.
  • Habitat: Place in the environment where an organism lives. E.g., mammalian intestines are habitats of tapeworms.
  • Biosphere: Surface of the earth where life exists (e.g., forests, mountains).
  • Ecological Niche: Role or mode of life of an organism within its habitat.
  • Ecosystem: Natural unit consisting of biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) components interacting to produce a stable system.
  • Community: Populations of plants and animals living together in a common environment.
  • Species: Group of organisms capable of interbreeding and producing viable offspring.

Habitat

Habitats are classified into two main types: aquatic and terrestrial habitats.

A) Aquatic Habitats

Type Description Examples of Inhabitants
Fresh Water Includes rivers and lakes Protozoans (amoeba), fish, aquatic plants like algae and papyrus
Marine (Salt Water) Includes seas, oceans, and swamps Sea anemones, sea weeds, whales, fish

B) Terrestrial Habitats

Type Inhabitants
Forests Birds, insects, fungi, monkeys
Savanna Birds, grazers, insects, grasses
Desert Xerophytes, cactus, euphorbia, camels
Underground Termites, burrowing mammals, earthworms

Environment

An organism in an environment is surrounded by different things. Therefore, the environment is divided into two:

  • Physical Environment: Non-living surroundings like temperature, soil, water, etc.
  • Living Environment: Other organisms around it like predators, prey, plants, and competitors.
