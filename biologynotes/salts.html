Natural Sources and Naming of Salts

2.1 Natural Sources of Salt

Natural sources of salt are sea water and rocks which consist of:

Compound Formula
Sodium chloride (common salt) NaCl
Sodium sulphate Na₂SO₄
Calcium sulphate CaSO₄
Calcium carbonate CaCO₃

Natural Sources of Specific Salts

Name of Salt Natural Sources
Sodium chloride Rock salt and sea water
Magnesium chloride Sea water and brine salt lakes (e.g., Great Salt Lake)
Magnesium sulphate Epsom salts
Sodium nitrate Chile saltpetre
Calcium carbonate Chalk, limestone, marble
Potassium chloride Sea water, potash

Naming of Salts from Different Acids

Acid and its Formula Class of Salt Examples
Hydrochloric acid (HCl) Chlorides Sodium chloride, Calcium chloride
Nitric acid (HNO₃) Nitrates Potassium nitrate, Lead(II) nitrate
Sulphuric acid (H₂SO₄) Sulphates Magnesium sulphate, Lithium sulphate
Sulphuric acid (H₂SO₄) Hydrogen sulphates Sodium hydrogen sulphate
Carbonic acid (H₂CO₃) Carbonates Zinc carbonate, Calcium carbonate
Carbonic acid (H₂CO₃) Hydrogen carbonates Calcium hydrogen carbonate
