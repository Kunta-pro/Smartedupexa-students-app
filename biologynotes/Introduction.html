Introduction to Biology

1.1 Meaning of Biology

Biology is a word derived from two Greek words: "Bios" meaning life and "Logos" meaning knowledge. It is a branch of science that deals with the study of life.

1.2 Biology in Everyday Life

Biology helps us understand our bodies and how they function. It enables us to prevent, cure, and eliminate diseases. Nutritionists use Biology to create balanced diets, while athletes use it to understand how their bodies work during exercise.

Biology also plays a crucial role in farming practices, environmental conservation, and biotechnology. Careers linked to Biology include medicine, pharmacy, and veterinary sciences.

1.3 Branches of Biology

Branch Description
Botany The study of plants.
Zoology The study of animals.
Ecology The study of organisms and their environment.
Genetics The study of inheritance.
Microbiology The study of microorganisms.

1.4 Characteristics of Living Things

  • Nutrition: The process by which living things obtain food for energy and growth.
  • Respiration: Breaking down food to release energy in the body.
  • Excretion: Removal of waste products from the body.
  • Reproduction: The ability of organisms to produce offspring.
  • Growth: A permanent increase in size of an organism.

1.5 Differences Between Plants and Animals

Plants Animals
Have a cell wall. Do not have a cell wall.
Have chlorophyll and make their own food. Feed on already-made food.
Growth occurs throughout life. Growth stops after maturity.
