1.1 Meaning of Biology

Biology is a word derived from two Greek words:

  • Bios: Meaning life
  • Logos: Meaning knowledge
Biology is a branch of science that deals with the study of life.

1.2 Biology in Everyday Life

Biology helps us in many ways:

  • Understanding our bodies and their functions.
  • Preventing, curing, and eliminating diseases through research.
  • Guiding on proper diets and exercise routines.
  • Understanding and conserving our environment.
  • Enhancing farming practices and ensuring food security.
  • Providing career opportunities like medicine, pharmacy, and veterinary sciences.

1.3 Branches of Biology

Branch Description
Botany Study of plants
Zoology Study of animals
Anatomy Study of the structure of living things
Genetics Study of inheritance
Ecology Study of how organisms relate to their environment

1.4 Characteristics of Living Things

  • Nutrition: Process of obtaining food for energy and growth.
  • Respiration: Breaking down food to release energy.
  • Excretion: Removing waste products from the body.
  • Reproduction: Giving rise to new organisms.
  • Movement: Transfer of parts of an organism in a given direction.
  • Locomotion: Whole organism moving from one place to another.
  • Growth: Permanent increase in size.
  • Irritability: Responding to stimuli in the environment.

Differences Between Plants and Animals

Plants Animals
Have a cell wall Lack a cell wall
Perform photosynthesis Feed on pre-made food
Growth occurs throughout life Growth stops after maturity

Life Forms of Organisms

Organisms can be unicellular or multicellular:

  • Unicellular: Made up of one cell (e.g., Amoeba, Bacteria).
  • Multicellular: Made up of many cells (e.g., Humans, Elephants).
