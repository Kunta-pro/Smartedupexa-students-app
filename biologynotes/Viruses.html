Viruses

5.1 Meaning of a Virus

A virus is a very small microscopic non-cellular particle that infects cells in order to multiply. They are made up of a genetic material (DNA or RNA) surrounded by a protein coat.

Viruses behave like living things in the following ways:

  • Like other living cells, a virus has genetic material.
  • Multiplies inside living cells.
  • Carry out metabolism inside living cells.
  • They can change (mutate) into new strains.

Also, viruses behave like non-living things in the following ways:

  • Not made up of cells.
  • Do not need to eat in order to live.
  • Need a host cell to multiply/reproduce.
  • Need a host cell to carry out metabolism.
  • Can crystallize & become inert if isolated from the host cell.

Viruses are not classified under any of the five kingdoms because they are non-living. However, though viruses are not living things, they exist in nature.

General Life Cycle of Viruses

The life cycle of a virus begins when it attaches to a host cell. Once inside, it uses the host's cellular machinery to replicate its genetic material and produce new virus particles. The new viruses are released from the host cell to infect other cells.

5.2 HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus)

HIV causes Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS). It weakens the immune system of the infected person.

Transmission of HIV

  • Having unprotected sex with an infected person.
  • Infected injections.
  • Blood transfusion from an infected person.
  • From an infected mother to her child during pregnancy.

Signs and Symptoms of AIDS

  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Rash
  • Sore throat
  • Weight loss
  • Cough

5.3 Hepatitis B

Hepatitis B is caused by the hepatitis B virus. It is transmitted when any fluid of an infected person enters the body of another person.

Signs and Symptoms of Hepatitis B

  • Yellowing of the skin and eyes
  • Dark urine
  • Feeling very tired
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Pain in the abdomen

5.4 Ebola

Ebola is caused by the Ebola virus and is transmitted through body contact with infected persons, such as kissing.

Signs and Symptoms of Ebola

  • Muscle and joint pain
  • Weight gain
  • Depression and anxiety
  • Memory loss
  • Trouble sleeping

5.5 Coronavirus (COVID-19)

COVID-19 is caused by the coronavirus. It is transmitted through air droplets.

Signs and Symptoms of COVID-19

  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Sore throat
  • Runny and blocked nose
  • Hoarse voice

5.6 Viral Infections in Plants

The cassava mosaic virus affects the tubers of the plant resulting in low crop yields. This virus causes the cassava mosaic disease and is transmitted by the whitefly.

Economic Importance of Viruses

Harmful Effects of Viruses

  • Kill cells and cause diseases (e.g., AIDS, Hepatitis B, Ebola, and Cassava Mosaic).
  • Weaken the immune system, making it easier for other diseases to attack (e.g., TB).

Beneficial Importance of Viruses

  • Used in biomedical research to insert new genes into cells.
  • Help in the process of evolution by transferring genes among species.
  • Some viruses protect the host against other infections (e.g., chickenpox).

Why Are Viruses Difficult to Destroy?

Viruses reproduce rapidly, do not respond to antibiotics, and constantly mutate into new strains. This makes them difficult to treat or vaccinate against, and viral pandemics can be highly harmful.

How the Knowledge of Biology Helps Combat Viral Pandemics

  • Biology helps us understand the causes, transmission, and symptoms of viral infections.
  • Biology aids in understanding the importance of balanced diets, good nutrition, and hygiene in managing viral diseases.
  • It highlights the impact of drug abuse and unsafe sexual behavior in the spread of viral infections.
  • Promotes cultural practices that do not hinder viral infection control, such as safer alternative rites of passage.
