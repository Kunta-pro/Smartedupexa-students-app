Nuclear Processes

Introduction

Nuclear chemistry is the study of the chemical and physical properties of elements which are influenced by the changes in the structure of the atomic nucleus.

The atomic nucleus is composed of protons and neutrons. The total number of protons in an element gives its atomic number (Z), while the sum of protons and neutrons gives the mass number (A) of a particular element.

Elements with unstable nuclei in their atoms can go through nuclear changes and turn into different elements. This process involves the nucleus in atoms and is therefore called a nuclear process.

Nuclear process is the process that involves fusing or splitting of the nucleus.

Nuclear Reactions

Introduction

A nuclear reaction is a process in which two nuclei or a nucleus and an external subatomic particle collide to produce one or more new nuclides.

A nuclear reaction must cause a transformation of at least one nuclide to another.

Radiation

Radiation is the emission or transmission of energy in the form of waves or particles through space.

Sunlight is the commonest radiation. However, there are other invisible radiations like radio waves and television waves.
